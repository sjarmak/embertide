# Ascension — Consolidated Rulebook

A single reference covering the core rules of *Ascension: Deckbuilding Game* plus every set-specific variation from the rulebooks of:

| # | Set | Role | Player count |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Chronicle of the Godslayer (**CotG**, 2nd Ed.) | Base game | 1–4 |
| 2 | Return of the Fallen (**RotF**) | 2-player standalone / expansion | 2 alone, up to 6 combined |
| 3 | Storm of Souls (**SoS**) | 1–4 standalone / expansion | 1–4 alone, up to 6 combined |
| 4 | Immortal Heroes (**IH**) | Expansion (designed for SoS) | 1–2 alone, up to 6 combined |
| 8 | Dawn of Champions (**DoC**) | Standalone | 2–4 alone, up to 6 combined |
| 9 | Dreamscape (**DRM**) | Standalone | 2–4 alone, up to 6 combined |

> **How to read this document.** The "Core Rules" sections describe the default game (as laid out in CotG). Any section headed **"Variation — [Set]"** is an explicit change, addition, or replacement rule introduced by that expansion. If multiple sets are played together, apply every variation whose set is in play; when two variations conflict, the card text always wins over the generic rule (see *Rules Priority*).

---

## 1. Overview

Ascension is a deckbuilding game. Each turn you play cards from your hand to produce **Runes (R)**, **Power (P)** and **Honor (H)**. Spend Runes to *acquire* Heroes and Constructs from the Center Row into your discard pile. Spend Power to *defeat* Monsters in the Center Row and gain their reward. Honor is the victory condition — the player with the most Honor when the Honor pool runs out wins.

## 2. Components (default game — CotG)

- 1 Game Board
- 50 Honor tokens (25 small clear = 1 H; 25 large red = 5 H)
- 200 Cards:
  - 4 personal starting decks of 10 (8 Apprentice + 2 Militia)
  - 60 "Always Available" cards: 1 Cultist, 30 Mystics, 29 Heavy Infantry
  - 100 Center Deck cards

### Variation — RotF (standalone, 2-player set)
- 30 Honor tokens; 120 cards; 2 starting decks; 35 Always Available (1 Cultist / 17 Mystic / 17 Heavy Infantry); 65 Center Deck cards.
- Can be mixed with CotG by shuffling both Center Decks together.

### Variation — SoS (standalone, 1–4)
- 50 Honor tokens; 200 cards; 4 starting decks; 59 Always Available including **6 Fanatics** (new); 101 Center Deck cards.

### Variation — IH (expansion)
- 30 Honor tokens; 184 cards; 2 starting decks; 35 Always Available (adds **2 Fanatics**); 69 Center Deck; **40 Soul Gem cards**; **20 "New Event" cards** (for the alternate Events variant).

### Variation — DoC (standalone)
- 40 **Reputation** tokens; 50 Honor tokens; 96 Center Deck cards; **4 oversized Champion cards**; **4 Champion Hero cards**; 4 starting decks; 53 Always Available (1 Cultist, 26 Mystic, 26 Heavy Infantry — *no Fanatics in this set*).

### Variation — DRM (Dreamscape, standalone)
- 50 Honor tokens; **30 Insight tokens (10 large 5-Insight, 20 small 1-Insight)**; 98 Center Deck; **35 Dream Deck cards** (gold-bordered); 4 starting decks; 41 Always Available (1 Cultist, 20 Mystic, 20 Heavy Infantry).

## 3. Setup (default)

1. Each player takes a 10-card starting deck (8 Apprentice + 2 Militia), shuffles it, and draws 5 cards.
2. Place the **Heavy Infantry**, **Mystic**, and **Cultist** stacks in a shared Always Available area.
3. Shuffle all remaining black-bordered cards into the **Center Deck**.
4. Flip 6 cards from the Center Deck face-up in a row to form the **Center Row**.
5. Reserve a spot next to the Center Row for the **Void** (discard/banish pile for Center Row cards).
6. Create the **Honor pool** for the game (see table below).
7. Randomly determine the first player. Play proceeds clockwise.

### Honor pool

| Players | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Honor (default) | 50 | 60 | 90 | 120 | 150 | 180 |

(5- and 6-player counts are only reachable when two or more sets are combined; RotF and SoS are the first sets to list them.)

### Variation — SoS / IH
- Add the **Fanatic** stack to the Always Available area during setup.

### Variation — DoC — Champions
- If playing with Champions, deal one oversized **Champion** card to each player (randomly or by agreement) and place it in front of them. Place the matching silver-bordered **Champion Hero** card beneath each Champion (it is added to the player's deck only when the Champion's Reputation Power acquires it). Give each player a starting pool of **Reputation (Rep) tokens** from the shared supply as needed.

### Variation — DRM — Dreamscape
- Before the game, deal each player **5 Dream cards** from the Dream Deck. Each player secretly chooses **3** to keep as their personal **Dreamscape** (face-down in front of them) and shuffles the other 2 back into the Dream Deck.
- Players may look at their own Dreamscape any time but must keep it hidden from opponents.
- Place the remaining Dream Deck to the side, accessible to all.

## 4. Card Types

### 4.1 Heroes
Played from your hand for a one-shot effect (Runes, Power, draw, banish, etc.). Any **Runes** or **Power** they generate stay in your pool until end of turn. Played Heroes remain in front of you until the end of your turn, then go to your discard pile.

### 4.2 Constructs
Played from your hand for a cost-free entry to play, and then stay in play across turns. Each ability on a Construct may be used once per turn (including the turn you play it). Constructs can be **destroyed** by card effects (sent to your discard pile).

### 4.3 Monsters
Defeated by spending Power equal to the number in their upper-right corner. When defeated, the Monster is banished to the Void and you gain its Reward (usually Honor plus an effect). Cards replacing the Monster come from the Center Deck before Monster rewards resolve (see *Timing*).

#### Variation — RotF — Fate cards
Some cards have a **Fate** keyword (white text on a black bar). When the card enters the Center Row — *including* when it is flipped during initial setup — its Fate effect triggers immediately. Replace the card in the Center Row if the Fate effect caused it to leave.

#### Variation — SoS — Events
- **Event** cards do not stay in the Center Row. When an Event flips off the Center Deck, move it immediately to the dedicated **Event Zone** on the board; then replace it in the Center Row as normal.
- Only **one** Event may occupy the Event Zone. When a new Event flips, the previous one is banished to the Void and the new Event becomes the **current Event**.
- An Event's text applies globally to every player while in play. Some cards have bonus effects keyed to the current Event's faction (shown as that faction's symbol in the card's text box).

#### Variation — SoS — Trophy Monsters
- When defeated, a Trophy Monster's **Honor reward is gained immediately**, but instead of being banished the Monster is placed **face-up in front of you** as a Trophy.
- On your turn, you may **banish** a Trophy to trigger its stored effect. A Trophy may be banished the turn it is gained or saved for a later turn.

#### Variation — SoS — Fanatic (Always Available Monster)
- The **Fanatic** is an Always Available Trophy Monster with a variable reward tied to the current Event (**Event Trophy**).
- You may hold **at most one Fanatic Trophy** at a time. If you defeat a Fanatic while already holding one, you gain the Honor reward but do **not** get a second Trophy.
- A banished Fanatic returns to the Always Available zone — it never goes to the Void.

#### Variation — IH — Ongoing Trophy Monsters
- Ongoing Trophy Monsters grant their Honor on defeat and are placed face-up in front of you, but are **not banished to use their effect** — the effect is continuous while the Trophy is in play.
- Example: *Kythis, Rebel Godling*: "Ongoing Trophy: Once per turn, gain a Soul Gem."

#### Variation — DoC — Faction Monsters
- Some Monsters have a **faction** symbol. They function identically to normal Monsters except they are affected by **Rally**, **Reputation Powers**, and any card that cares about faction.

#### Variation — DoC — Champion cards (optional)
- Each Champion is a persistent, oversized card in front of a player listing four **Reputation Powers**. Powers describe how that player gains **Reputation (Rep)** and the rewards unlocked at certain Rep thresholds.
- You gain **1 Rep** whenever you acquire or defeat a card of your Champion's faction (multifaction cards count if they share the Champion's faction).
- You may spend **Runes** to gain **Rep** once per turn (see the specific Champion for rate/cost).
- A Reputation Power that acquires the matching Champion Hero triggers as soon as you hit its Rep threshold, adding that silver-bordered Hero to your discard pile.
- Reputation thresholds that unlock ongoing effects (e.g., Rally effects) stay active for the rest of the game once activated.

#### Variation — DRM — Dreamborn Heroes / Constructs
- When a **Dreamborn** card enters the Center Row, **every** player gains **1 Insight**.
- When a Dreamborn card is **acquired**, the acquiring player gains **1 Insight**.

#### Variation — DRM — Vision cards (Dream Deck only)
- Visions are a new card type that live in each player's private Dreamscape.
- When a Vision is acquired from your Dreamscape, it is **not added to your deck**. Resolve its effect immediately and banish it — unless it has **Ongoing**, in which case it stays face-up in front of you and its effect persists for the rest of the game.

## 5. Keywords & Resources

### 5.1 Runes (R)
Produced by Heroes and Constructs. Spent to acquire Heroes/Constructs. Unspent Runes are lost at end of turn.

### 5.2 Power (P)
Produced by Heroes and Constructs. Spent to defeat Monsters. Unspent Power is lost at end of turn.

### 5.3 Honor (H)
The victory metric. Awarded by Monster rewards, printed on card bottom-left for acquired Heroes/Constructs.

### 5.4 Banish
Move a card to the Void. Defeated Monsters, and most cards banished from the Center Row or from a player's deck/hand/discard, go to the Void. Banishing weak starting-deck cards is a strategic tool — your deck "thins" toward the powerful cards you acquire.

- **Banished Always Available cards** (Mystic, Heavy Infantry, Cultist, Fanatic) return to their Always Available stack; they do **not** enter the Void.
- **Banished starting-deck cards** (Apprentice, Militia) are **removed from the game entirely** — they do not enter the Void.

### 5.5 Destroy
Move a Construct from play to its owner's **discard pile**. Introduced as a formal term in SoS; retroactively applies to any earlier effect that put a Construct into its owner's discard from play.

### 5.6 Unite (cross-set keyword)
A Hero with **Unite** has a bonus effect that triggers if you play another Hero of the **same faction** on the same turn. (Codified in later sets; applies to any Unite card regardless of expansion.)

### Variation — RotF — Fate
Card flips in the Center Row trigger an immediate effect. (See §4.3.)

### Variation — SoS — Event / Trophy / Destroy
See §4.3. Destroy is also promoted to a first-class keyword here.

### Variation — IH — Soul Gems
- Soul Gems are **not Heroes** and are **not** shuffled into the Center Deck. They form their own face-down shared pile beside the Always Available area.
- When a card effect tells you to "gain a Soul Gem," take the top card of the Soul Gem deck and place it **face-up** in front of you.
- You may play it that turn to gain its printed effect.
- At end of your turn, **any Soul Gems you still control are banished** (they cannot be saved for future turns). Because they are not Heroes, Soul Gems do **not** count for Unite or any "Hero" trigger.

### Variation — DoC — Rally
- **Rally** triggers when a card in the Center Row is replaced. The Rally effect names a characteristic (usually a faction). If the new card entering that slot has that characteristic, you may **acquire or defeat it without paying its cost**.
- Multiple simultaneous Rally effects stack: the newly-revealed card matches if it shares any characteristic among the active Rally effects. Rally effects do **not** carry over to subsequent reveals.

### Variation — DoC — Reputation (Rep)
- A player-specific resource introduced with Champions; carried turn-to-turn on your Champion card (like a score track).
- Gained by acquiring/defeating cards of your Champion's faction, by spending Runes per your Champion, or by specific card effects.
- Rep is **not** lost at end of turn. Spending/thresholds are defined on each Champion.

### Variation — DoC — Multifaction cards
- A Multifaction Hero or Construct counts as **all** factions listed on it simultaneously for every purpose (Rally, Unite, Reputation, card-text triggers).

### Variation — DRM — Insight (I)
- **Insight** is a new resource, separate from Runes and Power.
- Gained from card effects, Dreamborn triggers, and certain effects.
- **Unspent Insight is NOT lost at end of turn** — it carries over.
- Spent to acquire cards from your own **Dreamscape** (and to trigger Insight-cost abilities).

### Variation — DRM — Ongoing
- When a card with **Ongoing** is acquired or defeated (as appropriate), it stays face-up in front of you and its effect applies permanently until banished.

## 6. Turn Sequence

On your turn:

1. **Play cards** from your hand (any number, any order) to generate Runes, Power, Honor, and trigger effects.
2. **Acquire** Heroes/Constructs (spend Runes) and/or **defeat** Monsters (spend Power). Any number, in any order, as long as you have the resources.
3. **End step:** place any remaining cards in your hand, plus all Heroes played this turn, into your discard pile. Played Constructs stay in play. Lose any unspent Runes and Power.
4. **Draw 5 cards** from your personal deck. If your deck runs out mid-draw, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck, then continue drawing.

Play passes clockwise.

### Variation — DRM
Step 2 also allows spending **Insight** to acquire cards from your own Dreamscape. Insight is kept across turns.

### Variation — DoC
During your turn you may also spend Runes to gain Reputation once (if your Champion allows it), and any Reputation-threshold rewards trigger automatically the moment you cross their threshold.

---

## 7. Acquiring Heroes & Constructs

- To acquire a card from the Center Row, spend Runes equal to its cost.
- The card goes to your **discard pile**.
- **Replace the empty slot** from the Center Deck **immediately**, before anything else resolves. The Center Row should always have 6 cards when a player is making a decision.
- **Mystics** and **Heavy Infantry** are Always Available and may be acquired any number of times per turn (while supply remains).

### Variation — SoS / IH
A replacement card that is an **Event** is moved to the Event Zone and another card is flipped to fill the Center Row. The old Event is banished if one was already present.

### Variation — DoC — Rally replacement
If a Rally effect matches the incoming card, you may acquire that card for **0** (or defeat it, if a Monster, for 0 Power).

### Variation — DRM — Dreamscape acquisition
- You may acquire cards from **your own Dreamscape only** (not opponents').
- Dreamscape acquisition uses **Insight** instead of Runes.
- Effects that let you acquire Heroes "without paying their cost" (e.g., Druids of the Stone Circle from earlier sets) **do not** work on Dreamscape cards — you may only interact with the Dreamscape via cards that explicitly mention it.

---

## 8. Defeating Monsters

- Spend Power equal to the Monster's defeat cost (upper-right).
- Banish the Monster to the Void and replace its slot from the Center Deck **before** resolving the reward.
- Collect the Reward (Honor + any other effect). Honor tokens come from the Honor pool to your personal score pile.
- The **Cultist** is Always Available, is never banished to the Void, and can be defeated as many times as you can pay for in one turn.

### Variation — SoS — Trophy Monsters
See §4.3. Honor is gained on defeat; the Trophy stays in front of you for later banishment-for-effect.

### Variation — SoS — Fanatic
Always Available Monster; Trophy reward varies by the current Event (Event Trophy); max 1 Fanatic Trophy per player; banished Fanatic returns to the Always Available zone.

### Variation — IH — Ongoing Trophy
Ongoing Trophies give their effect continuously while in play; they are **not** banished to activate.

### Variation — DoC — Faction Monsters & Rally
Monsters can have a faction; Rally and Reputation interact with them normally.

### Variation — DoC — Velrath, Lord of Deofol (Ongoing Trophy, exception case)
While you control Velrath, Lord of Deofol, you may spend Power to "defeat" **Heroes in the Center Row** as if they were Monsters. On defeat you gain Honor equal to the Hero's printed Honor, gain the Hero's effect (but it does **not** count as having played the Hero for any other trigger), and the Hero is banished.

### Variation — DRM — Dreamborn on defeat
When a **Dreamborn Monster** enters the Center Row, every player gains 1 Insight; when acquired/defeated, the acquiring/defeating player gains 1 Insight.

---

## 9. Ending a Turn / Replenishing Your Hand

- Discard played Heroes and any leftover cards in hand.
- Keep played Constructs in play (they carry over).
- Lose unspent Runes, Power; *keep* unspent Insight (DRM) and Reputation (DoC).
- Draw 5 cards from your deck (reshuffling discard into deck if needed).

---

## 10. Game End & Scoring

- The game ends **at the end of the round** in which the final Honor token is taken from the pool. "Round" = completing the cycle back to the starting player (so every player has played an equal number of turns).
- Continue to award Honor for rewards and other effects during the final round even after the pool is empty — track the excess with leftover tokens.
- Final score: sum all Honor tokens you've taken **plus** the Honor values printed on every card you still own (hand + deck + discard + Constructs in play). Banished and removed cards score nothing.
- Highest total wins. **Ties:** the tied player who started later wins (starting player loses all ties, the 2nd player loses to the 3rd/4th, etc.).

### Variation — Trophies in play at game end
Trophies and Ongoing Trophies in front of you at game end do not have a printed Honor value, so they score 0 unless their card text says otherwise.

### Variation — Soul Gems at game end
Soul Gems in front of you are banished at end of turn and never enter your deck; they never score.

## 11. General Rules & Timing

1. **Deck empty.** Any time you need to draw or reveal from an empty deck, shuffle your discard pile into a new deck.
2. **Center Deck empty.** Shuffle the **Void** to form a new Center Deck.
3. **Card text beats the rulebook.** If a card's text contradicts a generic rule, follow the card.
4. **Replace first.** When a Center Row card leaves for any reason (acquire, defeat, banish, Fate banish), refill the Center Row from the Center Deck **before** resolving any rewards or triggers. The Center Row should hold 6 cards at every decision point.
5. **Resolve-in-sequence order for "immediate" effects** (from CotG FAQ, extended by later sets):
   1. The Center Row card is acquired or defeated (goes to your discard pile or to the Void).
   2. Replace cards in the Center Row until there are 6, working outward from the Center Deck side.
      - *SoS+:* Any **Event** revealed during refill moves to the Event Zone (banishing the previous Event first).
   3. Apply Monster rewards.
   4. Resolve any triggers keyed to defeating/acquiring the card (e.g., Voidthirster).
   5. *RotF+:* Resolve any **Fate** triggers on newly-revealed Center Row cards, in the order they entered (left-to-right / Center-Deck-outward).

### Variation — SoS — Hero cards replayed / returned
If a Construct (or Hero) leaves play and re-enters play on the same turn, it's a "new" card for ability-use purposes — its once-per-turn abilities reset.

### Variation — DRM — Dreamborn Insight timing
Players do not gain Insight from a Dreamborn card entering the Center Row until **after** the triggering effect (e.g., the Monster reward that caused the reveal) finishes resolving.

## 13. Gameplay Variants

### 13.1 Solitaire (1 Player)

Shared across all sets, with the antagonist renamed per set's theme:

- Set aside **50 Honor** tokens.
- Lay out the Center Row normally. When a Center Row card leaves, the replacement **always enters on the left** and the remaining cards slide right.
- You take the first turn. At the end of **each of your turns**, the AI opponent is considered to acquire or defeat the **two rightmost cards** of the Center Row.
- AI-acquired cards are set aside and only counted for the AI's Honor total.
- AI-defeated Monsters go to the Void and award **only** the Honor portion of their reward to the AI; all other effects are ignored.
- Continue until the Honor pool is exhausted; compare totals. If your Honor is higher, you win.

| Set | Solitaire opponent name |
|---|---|
| CotG, RotF | Samael |
| SoS | Nemesis |
| DoC, DRM | The Cult |

### 13.2 Team Play (4 or 6 players, teams of 2)

- 30 Honor tokens per player (so 120 / 180 total).
- Teammates sit across the table from each other.
- Your teammate is **not** harmed by the penalty side of Monster rewards you inflict (e.g., a defeated Vaultbreaker Wurm / Corrosive Widow / Sea Tyrant / Noxious Soul / Hulking Crab forces only *opponents* to lose a card, not your teammate).
- You may spend **1 additional Rune** when acquiring a card to place that card into your **teammate's** discard pile instead of your own.
- Final score is the sum of the team's Honor. Highest team total wins.

### 13.3 Alternate Event Rule (IH — optional)

When mixing IH with older sets (especially CotG + RotF, which predate Events), Events can appear too infrequently. The alternate variant:

1. Remove **all Event cards** from every Center Deck and shuffle them together into a separate face-down **Event stack** next to the Event Zone.
2. IH ships **20 "New Event"** proxy cards. Shuffle **5 of them per additional set in play** into the Center Deck (e.g., CotG + SoS + IH → shuffle in 15; CotG + RotF + SoS + IH → shuffle in 20).
3. Whenever a "New Event" card flips from the Center Deck, reveal the **top card of the Event stack** and treat it as if it had flipped — move it into the Event Zone, banishing any previous Event.
4. **Optional additional rule:** start the game with a random Event from the Event stack already in play.

## 14. Glossary

- **Always Available** — Cards outside the Center Row that any player may always acquire/defeat (Mystic, Heavy Infantry, Cultist; plus Fanatic in SoS/IH).
- **Banish** — Place into the Void (or, for Always Available cards, back to their stack; for starting-deck cards, out of the game).
- **Center Deck / Center Row / Void** — Shared supply (deck), 6 face-up market (row), banishment pile (Void).
- **Champion** — Oversized leader card granting Reputation Powers (DoC only).
- **Construct** — Card that stays in play across turns; abilities usable once per turn.
- **Destroy** — Move a Construct from play to its owner's discard pile.
- **Dreamborn** — Hero/Construct type that gives Insight on entering the Center Row and on acquisition (DRM).
- **Dreamscape** — A player's private 3-card stack purchased with Insight (DRM).
- **Event** — Global rules-modifying card in the Event Zone (SoS, IH).
- **Fanatic** — Always Available Trophy Monster with an Event-dependent reward (SoS, IH).
- **Fate** — On-entry trigger for a Center Row card (RotF+).
- **Hero** — One-shot card played from hand; discarded at end of turn.
- **Honor (H)** — The victory resource.
- **Insight (I)** — Resource spent on Dreamscape purchases; persists across turns (DRM).
- **Monster** — Center Row card defeated by Power; banished on defeat unless it's a Trophy.
- **Multifaction** — A card counts as every faction listed on it (DoC+).
- **Ongoing** — A card whose effect persists in play after acquisition/defeat (DRM, and on certain Trophies in IH).
- **Power (P)** — Resource for defeating Monsters.
- **Rally** — A one-shot effect that triggers when the Center Row is replaced; lets you acquire/defeat the new card free if it matches (DoC).
- **Reputation (Rep)** — Persistent player-specific resource on Champion cards (DoC).
- **Runes (R)** — Resource for acquiring Heroes/Constructs.
- **Soul Gem** — Face-up non-Hero card gained by effects and banished at end of turn (IH).
- **Trophy** — A defeated Monster held in play; banishable on your turn for its effect (SoS).
- **Unite** — Bonus effect that triggers from playing a same-faction Hero on the same turn.
- **Vision** — Dream Deck card type with an immediate effect on acquisition; banished unless Ongoing (DRM).
- **Void** — The shared banish pile; reshuffled into the Center Deck if the Center Deck is exhausted.

## 15. Rules Priority (when combining sets)

1. **Card text > rulebook.** Always follow specific card text.
2. **Newer clarifications > older.** Where a later set explicitly re-defines a keyword (e.g., SoS formalizing **Destroy**; SoS renaming CotG's "put a Construct into your discard" as Destroy), apply the newer wording retroactively.
3. **Current Event modifies all players.** In a combined game with SoS/IH Events, the active Event's text is part of every player's rules context until replaced.
4. **Starting decks and Always Available cards remain per-set components** (they do not merge): Apprentice/Militia from one set can be banished *out of the game*; Always Available cards return to their specific stacks.
5. **Player counts:** use the highest applicable Honor target — so a combined 2-player CotG+RotF game still plays to 60 Honor; a 5-player combined game plays to 150 Honor; a 6-player to 180.

## 17. Selected Rules Clarifications (from the FAQs of each rulebook)

### CotG

- **Center Row replacement timing.** When you defeat a Monster with an effect like *Mephit* ("banish a card in the center row"), the Mephit is replaced **first**, then you choose your banish target.
- **Cultist is unlimited.** You may defeat the Cultist as many times as you can afford on one turn.
- **Honor pool running out** does **not** end the game immediately — the current round finishes, and additional Honor tokens from reserve continue to be awarded.
- **Tablet of Time's Dawn** grants an extra turn when banished from play; if you banish it on the final turn, you still take the extra turn.
- **Cost-matching acquire cards** (Druids of the Stone Circle, Arha Templar, etc.) can only acquire/defeat cards **within the listed cost** — you cannot combine them with other Runes/Power to acquire a more expensive card.
- **Heavy Infantry / Mystic supply is finite.** Once their stacks are exhausted, they can no longer be acquired.
- **Banishing a card from your hand** does **not** trigger its effect; playing it first and then trying to banish it during that same turn is impossible because played cards are in front of you, not in your hand, until end of turn.
- **Velrath, Duke of Veils** — stolen cards stay permanently in the stealing player's deck; victims do **not** draw a replacement.

### RotF
- Fate triggers on initial deal. If the opening Center Row contains Fate cards, resolve them in reading order (leftmost first) before the first player's turn.

### SoS
- **Arha Rising** only applies to players on their own turn — forced discards on opponents' turns are unaffected.
- **Construct leaving and returning to play** resets once-per-turn abilities.
- **Hedron Rising + Fanatic Event Trophy.** You may banish the Fanatic to save your Construct from destruction as long as Hedron Rising is the *active* Event at the moment the Construct would be destroyed. If a new Event replaces Hedron Rising first, the save is lost.
- **You cannot banish a Fanatic to satisfy your own Construct-destruction costs** (e.g., Scrap Flinger, Autobuilder 5.0) — if the cost says "destroy a Construct," you must actually destroy it, and Hedron Rising's preventive effect does not apply.
- **Fanatic is never in the Void.** Even if an effect returns Monsters from the Void, you can't retrieve a Fanatic from there.
- **Reveal-with-empty-deck.** If a card effect requires you to reveal from an empty deck, first reshuffle your discard pile, then reveal.
- **Void Rising & Monster defeats.** Void Rising's Honor bonus requires it to be the current Event at the moment you defeat the Monster; if it flips *after* defeat it doesn't apply, but if it was current *during* defeat, a later replacement Event doesn't revoke the bonus.
- **Vandal Soul tie-break.** The controller of the Construct being destroyed chooses which one to destroy when multiple tie.

### IH
- **Soul Gems disappear at end of turn** — they cannot be saved. They are not Heroes (no Unite triggers).
- **Nightfall's Event Trophy** lets you name a Hero; copies of that Hero are Lifebound for the rest of the turn, in addition to their other factions.
- **Well of Souls Event Trophy** lets you discard a card to gain a Soul Gem — only if you actually discard (no empty-hand cheat).
- **Growmite Ongoing Trophies** stack. Each Growmite Trophy you have gives the extra Honor for each other Growmite defeated — you can keep chain-killing them for compounding Honor.
- **Amnesiac Constructs.** A Construct that leaves and re-enters play during the same turn resets its once-per-turn uses.
- **Well of Souls makes Monsters Unbanishable**, so effects like Ravenous Gorph's Fate will resolve but won't successfully banish anything.

### DoC
- **Multiple Rally effects** stack for a single reveal. A revealed card triggers if it matches *any* of the active Rally characteristics. Rally does not carry over past that single reveal.
- **Reputation thresholds apply instantly.** Crossing the Rep threshold for an ongoing Rally effect (e.g., 8 Rep) activates it on the very card that pushed you over.
- **Multifaction + Reputation.** Acquiring any card sharing your Champion's faction grants Rep (including multifaction cards).
- **Conversion Port** makes all Constructs count as every faction — all Rally effects and all Construct-faction Rep gains trigger for every Construct acquired.
- **Premonition Bell** cannot copy a Hero whose card text explicitly forbids copying (e.g., Daybreak Askara).
- **Velrath, Lord of Deofol** lets you "defeat" Center Row Heroes: gain their printed Honor, then their one-shot effect as if played, then banish them. It does **not** count as having played the Hero (so triggers like Mantra of Renewal, or Daybreak Askara copies, do not fire), and on-acquire triggers (like Undaunted Scout's Rally) do **not** fire either.

### DRM (Dreamscape)
- **Dreamborn Insight gain resolves after rewards.** If a Dreamborn card enters the Center Row as a result of a Monster reward (like Puppetmaster taking Insight from opponents), the Insight gain happens *after* the current effect finishes — so if the opponent had no Insight when the reward resolved, the Dreamborn gain doesn't retroactively give them any to take.
- **Copying a Hero's effect** (e.g., Aura of the Askara's cost reduction) does **not** count as playing a second physical copy — you cannot acquire Aura of the Askara for free by copying its own effect.
- **Nairi's Blessing** does count as a Lifebound Hero play for "played Hero" counters.
- **Cross-set "acquire without paying cost"** effects (e.g., Druids of the Stone Circle) cannot target Dreamscape cards — only cards that explicitly mention the Dreamscape can interact with it.
