## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with leaded stone pillars left and right entwined with curling moss and ivy tendrils, trefoil quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels, soft sage-green leaf motifs along a plain leaded inner border (regular-enemy treatment). Subject: the Squidlet — a small alert woodland cephalopod settled centered in the arch at about sixty percent of arch height, a creature built entirely from a bulb-and-tentacles plan with no separate torso, head, belly, legs, or feet. Design it from first principles: (1) the dominant visual mass is a LARGE ROUNDED CAMOUFLAGE BULB-MANTLE, like a mossy stump-cap, mottled with overlapping patches of moss-green, sage, and forest-teal, its surface softly segmented into glass panes; (2) EIGHT soft tentacles splay directly out from beneath the bulb in a radial heraldic spread, sage-green with subtle pale-cream sucker-cups along their undersides, curling outward across a small mossy stone-and-root ledge — the tentacle-skirt is the entire lower silhouette and NOTHING shows beneath it (no body, no legs, no feet); (3) a small face sits on the front of the bulb itself with TWO round glowing amber-orange jewel-cabochon eyes, each pierced by a single THICK CHUNKY HORIZONTAL black slit-pupil — a bold flat black bar running side-to-side across roughly a third of the eye's height (never a thin hairline, never a round dot, never a vertical slit); below them a small puckered circular dark mouth, ready to spit a leaf-wrapped pebble. Alert and faintly whimsical, not scary. Our gilding motif touches it lightly for a low-tier creature: a few hairline gold fracture-lines vein the crown of the bulb where the Embertide's overflow has begun to gild it, the panel-seams traced in warm gold filigree, and a faint amber inner shimmer glints at those cracks. Background: a minimal centered rose-window suggestion behind the subject with abstract forest-canopy shapes and faint leaf-and-vine filigree at the edges, NOT a literal forest scene, deep cobalt-night accents in the outer border. Gentle magical lighting with soft rays and ambient glow radiating from the subject, no harsh shadows. Thick dark leading lines separating segments, painterly gradients within each pane. Highly readable simplified silhouette. Colors permitted: moss-green, sage, forest-teal, camo-patch lighter-green, glowing amber-orange jewel-eye, chunky-black slit-pupil, cracked-gold hairline fracture-lines, warm-gold filigree seam, faint-amber crack-shimmer, pale-cream sucker, deep-cobalt-night border, dark-leading-outline. Do NOT render: any body, torso, belly, legs, or feet beneath or behind the tentacles; an armored shell-helm or riveted metal plates; a single cyclops eye; round dot pupils; vertical slit pupils; thin hairline pupils. The pupils MUST be thick chunky horizontal slit-bars; the creature MUST have tentacles, not legs. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel filling the square canvas.
