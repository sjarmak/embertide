## Prompt

Stained glass landscape panel, 21:9 panoramic format, Elysian Cathedral card-art style: a cursed sacred-stone pit half-corrupted by gloom, seen through a leaded-glass window. A weathered tile arena floor stretches across the foreground, with a low layer of pale-purple gloom-mist pooling and drifting across the stone. Cursed runic markings glow soft red through the cracks in the floor in a ring around the center of the arena. From the deep cracks in the floor, faint lava-amber glow seeps upward through the haze. The far wall arches into a tall sacred-stone gateway, its top half corroded to dark obsidian by the gloom while the bottom half retains its original weathered cream stone — the line of corruption visible across the arch. Behind the gateway, an ominous deep-red and obsidian sky simmers. Gloom-purple and lava-red dominant with obsidian-corroded gateway architecture and weathered cream-stone accents on the surviving sacred stonework. Thick dark leading lines separating glass segments, painterly stained-glass tone variation within each segment. Background reads as the fourth tier of the colosseum — heavy, hells-gate stakes, demonic-revival pressure on sacred ground. Composition is intentionally low-density in the center: the arena floor and middle ground are mostly empty so that boss-card portraits placed on top remain the visual hero. No central foreground objects: no shards, no thrones, no pillars, no columns, no statues, no debris in the central arena — only the empty corrupted floor with its glowing crack-veins. No monsters, no characters, no figures of any kind in the panel. No text, no letters, no captions, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the 21:9 canvas.
