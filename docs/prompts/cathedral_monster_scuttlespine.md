## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with leaded sandstone pillars left and right entwined with dusty-amber desert vines and tiny glass-panel sun-bleached leaves, trefoil quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels with small lapis-lazuli inlay accents, plain leaded inner border (regular-enemy treatment). Subject: the Scuttlespine — a massive armored desert centipede centered in the arch, its long heavily-segmented body coiling through the dunes in a dramatic S-curve, partially submerged so several coil-humps surface above the sandy base while the head segment rears upward to about sixty-five percent of arch height. The body is a chain of ten-plus segmented plates running from head to coiled tail, each plated in heavy weathered terracotta-and-cream chitin with hardened ridges along the dorsal spine. Every segment carries a pair of sharp angular chitinous legs bristling from the underside — predatory sand-armored legs, not soft nubs — kicking up sand where the body breaches the dunes. The broad front head-segment is dramatically armored with twin menacing feelers curving back from the upper edges and jagged pinching mandibles protruding forward. A single giant glowing red-orange eye-orb dominates the front of the head, taking up the majority of its face, the iris a luminous red-orange-amber gradient with no pupil, glaring forward like a spotlight; no other eyes anywhere on the creature. Our gilding motif corrodes the carapace: hairline gold fracture-lines branch across the terracotta plates with a faint warm-amber glow leaking from the segment-seams, brightest around the great eye, as though the chitin is being slowly turned to gilt. A violent dust-cloud of cascading sand-grains and rock fragments explodes from the burrow-points. Background: warm sandstone-tan panels with dusk-rose haze rays angling down, terracotta-orange dune ridges along the lower band, a lapis-lazuli sky band along the upper edge, a faint distant vault-silhouette in the haze, thick dark leading lines separating each segment, painterly tone variation within each pane. Warm sun-washed cathedral lighting from upper left on the stone arch frame only. Colors permitted: cream-and-terracotta-armor, sandstone-tan, terracotta-orange-segment, deep-segment-seam-shadow, jagged-chitin-leg-shadow, glowing-red-orange eye-orb, red-orange-eye-halo, cracked-gold fracture-lines, warm-amber gild-glow, ivory-mandible-pincer, lapis-lazuli-sky, dusk-rose-haze, dark-leading-outline. The creature must read as a single-eyed armored centipede, not a serpent or dragon. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the square canvas.
