## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with leaded stone pillars left and right entwined with emerald ivy and tiny glass-panel leaves, trefoil quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels, plain leaded inner border (regular-enemy treatment). Subject: the Snapvine — a predatory carnivorous plant centered in the arch, a thick coiling emerald vine-stalk rising from below the frame and terminating at about sixty-five percent of arch height in a huge snapping flower-mouth reared back mid-strike. The mouth is a bloom of curling petals lined with jagged fang-thorns around a glowing crimson gullet and a thick lashing tongue-frond; barbed leaves curl outward from the stem like jagged armor. Our gilding motif corrupts the plant: hairline gold fracture-lines branch up the vine-stalk and across the petals with a faint warm-amber glow leaking from the cracks, the corruption brightest at the throat where it meets the crimson gullet, as though the living wood is being slowly turned to gilt. Background: deep forest-green and moss-green stained-glass panels with dappled light rays streaming diagonally through an unseen canopy, thick dark leading lines separating each segment, painterly tone variation within each pane. Warm sun-washed cathedral lighting from upper left on the stone arch frame only, the interior kept cool forest-toned. Colors permitted: emerald vine, moss-green leaf, crimson gullet, fang-thorn cream, cracked-gold fracture-lines, warm-amber gild-glow, forest-green background, dappled-gold light-ray, dark-leading-outline. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the square canvas.
