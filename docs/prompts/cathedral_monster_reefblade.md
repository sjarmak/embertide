Subject: the Reefblade — a hostile armored bivalve predator centered in the arch at about sixty-five percent of arch height, viewed from a slight three-quarter angle, its heavy ridged shell cracked open along a horizontal seam in a snapping-attack pose. The upper shell-half is hinged up like the roof of a mouth, the lower half forms the base, and along the seam the shell-edges are jagged into interlocking triangular shell-teeth — bony spikes alternating from the upper and lower halves like a jaw of carapace; these are the creature's only teeth. The two outer shell-halves are weathered cobalt-teal stone-coral with concentric ribbed ridges flowing from the hinged back forward to the toothed rim, barnacle clusters along the underside, pearly iridescence on the dorsal ridge. Our gilding motif corrodes the shell: hairline gold fracture-lines branch across the cobalt ridges with a faint warm-amber glow leaking from the deepest cracks, the corruption creeping toward the hinge. Inside the open mouth, past the stretching cord, the interior recedes into dark shadow with hints of pale-pink tissue lining the walls. The shell sits forward-tilted on a flat sandy seabed shelf.
