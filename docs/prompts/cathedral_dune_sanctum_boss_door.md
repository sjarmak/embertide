Tightly framed with no background scenery, no landscape, no sky. Subject: a massive closed sandstone-vault DOUBLE DOOR in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style — arched gothic doorway filling ~90% of the square canvas, facing the viewer straight-on and CLOSED (no gap between the two door leaves), its lower courses half-swallowed by drifted gold sand. The stone arch frame around the door is warm sandstone-tan and dusk-rose basalt, sun-weathered, with bold hieroglyphic Duneborn-spiral motifs carved into the keystone above and dusty-amber wind-worn fissures in the spandrels. Lapis-lazuli inlay borders run along every edge of the arch, the door-frame, and the crossbeams — clean rich blue lines tracing the architecture. Small drifting sand grains float in front of the stone arch. At the dead-center meeting point of the two door leaves, a LARGE prominent boss-key keyhole set directly into a SUN-BLEACHED HOURGLASS-OF-FALLING-SAND POOL embedded in the door's surface: an irregular hourglass-shape carved into the door, with bright cascading sand pouring downward through the narrow waist from an upper basin to a lower basin, lit from within by a warm-amber sun-glow that suggests light pooling through the falling grains — absolutely NO fan-shape, NO shell-shape, NO scale-pattern, NO molten-magma, NO coral. The hourglass-sand pool is roughly thirty percent of the door width, organic and asymmetric in its carved outline (not a neat geometric hourglass — a weathered irregular sand-cascade pocket as if the desert itself is bleeding light through the stone). The keyhole itself sits at the dead center of the hourglass waist where the sand cascades through (circular top with vertical slot below), cut cleanly into a raised sandstone-cream lip, with the warm sun-amber glow pouring outward from the keyhole opening. A thin gold filigree tracery runs along the outer arch, glinting warm in the desert sun. The door is IMPASSABLY SEALED — no light leaks through, no interior visible. Style: painterly stained-glass illustration with thick dark leading outlines separating each stone block, bronze band, and the hourglass-pool edge. Dramatic directional cathedral lighting from upper left catching the warm sandstone surface; warm-amber underglow in the sun-cracks and in the hourglass-sand pool + keyhole. Colors permitted: warm sandstone-tan, sun-bleached cream, dusk-rose, terracotta-orange, lapis-lazuli accent blue, dusk-bronze metal, dusty-amber haze, warm-amber sun-glow, gold-filigree-accent, sand-cream highlight, dark-leading-outline. ABSOLUTELY NO green anywhere on the door, arch, hourglass-sand pool, or ornament; green is ONLY the flat background behind the subject. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no cathedral pews, no landscape — ONLY the closed boss door with its organic sun-bleached hourglass-of-falling-sand keyhole centerpiece, on a pure solid uniform #00FF00 green backdrop.
