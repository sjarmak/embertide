## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with leaded stone pillars left and right blackened by soot and veined with glowing ember-red cracks, trefoil quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels, plain leaded inner border with soft ember-amber filigree (regular-enemy treatment). Subject: the Cinder-Mantle — a small ember-corrupted cephalopod creature, centered in the arch at about sixty percent of arch height, viewed front-on like a collectible portrait. Its body is a single rounded MANTLE-BULB of fused volcanic slag, mottled charcoal-black and ember-crimson, the surface broken into glass-panel facets by our gilding motif: a branching web of hairline gold fracture-lines creeping over the bulb with a faint warm amber light leaking from the seams, as though molten metal is corroding the creature from inside out. From directly beneath the bulb spread EIGHT curling tentacles in a radial heraldic skirt — the bulb-mantle is the whole body, classic cephalopod anatomy with no separate torso, no legs, no feet; nothing shows beneath the tentacle-skirt. The tentacles are scarlet-red shading to gilt-cracked tips, pale-amber sucker-cups along their undersides, coiling outward in graceful symmetry, the lowest curls resting on a cracked-basalt ledge that leaks faint ember-glow from its fissures. On the front of the bulb sits a small simple face: two luminous amber-orange jewel-cabochon eyes, each crossed by a single bold thick horizontal black slit-pupil (a heavy flat band running side to side, never a round dot, never a thin hairline), and below them a small puckered circular dark mouth-vent ready to expel a glowing ember-pebble, a thread of smoke curling from it. Background: stained-glass panels in deep-crimson and volcanic-amber arranged in a concentric heat-bloom behind the subject, abstract vent-shapes and faint ember-and-fissure filigree at the edges, thick dark leading lines separating each segment, painterly tone variation within each pane, radiating heat-wave rays, scattered volcanic-ash flecks frozen in the glass. Warm sun-washed cathedral lighting from upper left on the stone arch frame only, the interior kept hot-toned with the bulb's inner gild-glow as a secondary light source. Colors permitted: charcoal-black, ember-crimson, scarlet-red, volcanic-amber, cracked-gold fracture-lines, warm amber inner-glow, pale-amber sucker-cups, deep-crimson background, smoldering-ash flecks, dark-leading-outline. The creature must have tentacles, not legs; the pupils must be thick horizontal slit-bars. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the square canvas.
