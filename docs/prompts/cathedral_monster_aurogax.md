## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with leaded stone pillars left and right veined by thin glowing gold fracture-lines, trefoil and quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels, and a region-boss rose-window crowning the upper arch — a dense radial wheel of gold-leaf tracery, the densest ornamentation of the bestiary, signalling a super-boss. Subject: Aurogax, the Three-Crowned Wyrm — a single colossal serpentine wyrm centered low in the arch, one heavy coiled body of ash-grey scales from which three long sinuous necks rise in a clean radial fan, broad-shouldered and powerful with strong vertical symmetry, at about sixty-five percent of the arch height. The whole wyrm is gripped by the gilding motif: cracked gold-gilt corrosion creeps in broad plaques over the chest, shoulders, and along the spine, hairline gold fracture-lines branch across the ash-scaled hide between the plaques, and a faint amber inner glow leaks from deep in the cracks as though molten light is caged inside the body. The scaled hide is solid and unbroken — no exposed bone, no visible ribs, no skeletal anatomy; just ash-grey scale armored with gilt. Thin ribbons of pale-gold vapor and smoke curl from the three jaws. Each of the THREE HEADS sits on its own long neck, well separated and distinct, sharing the same dragon-skull proportions but each ringed by its own CROWN of dense gilding-fracture — a halo of cracked gold spreading from the brow back over the crest like a corroded diadem, so the trio reads as one wyrm wearing three crowns of the same corruption. CRITICAL — every head has EXACTLY ONE LARGE prominent EYE: a single oversized eye centered on the side of each ash-grey snout, a smooth amber orb glowing with the inner-furnace light, never paired, never two eyes per head, one bold eye per head. The three crowns differ only in the density and spread of their gold-fracture haloes (left sparse and jagged, center broad and full, right fine and lace-like) so the heads read as a unified trio carrying the same gilding while remaining individual. Beneath the wyrm, a low drift of pale-gold vapor seeps outward across the arch floor. Background: stained-glass panels in deep-indigo and smoldering-amber arranged as a concentric corruption-bloom of fracturing gold rings radiating from the body, harmonized through thick dark leading, painterly tone variation within each pane, an amber rim-light tracing the silhouette. Colors permitted: ash-grey wyrm-scale, cracked gold-gilt corrosion, gold fracture-lines and fracture-crowns, warm amber inner-glow and eyes, pale-gold vapor and smoke, deep-indigo background, smoldering-amber pane, amber rim-highlight, dark-leading-outline. Heraldic three-headed centerpiece pose, broad-bodied and centered with strong vertical symmetry, the three necks rising in a clean radial fan. The gilt-corruption gripping the wyrm IS the design. No exposed bone, no ribs, no ribcage, no skeletal anatomy — solid scaled hide only. No human figure, no humanoid face, no paired eyes on any head — strictly one large eye per head. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no UI overlays, no banners, no ribbons, no scrolls, no inscriptions of any kind — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the square canvas.
