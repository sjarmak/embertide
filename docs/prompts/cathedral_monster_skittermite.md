## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with leaded stone pillars left and right blackened by soot and veined with glowing ember-red cracks, trefoil quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels, plain leaded inner border (regular-enemy treatment). Subject: the Skittermite — a small leaping ember-arachnid centered in the arch at about sixty percent of arch height, caught mid-pounce in a star-pattern pose with strong fourfold symmetry. Its body is a rounded charred-shell dome, and a single enormous vertically-slit amber eye dominates the front of it, glowing from within. Four long thin spring-legs splay outward like a leaping star, each ending in a small curved claw-tip; the dome-body is the whole creature, no separate head, no torso below the legs. Our gilding motif corrupts the shell: hairline gold fracture-lines branch across the dome and down the legs with a faint warm-amber glow leaking from the cracks, brightest around the great eye, as though the carapace is being slowly gilded from inside. A thin wisp of ember-smoke trails from the rear of the dome. Background: crimson and deep-amber stained-glass panels with rays radiating from the eye and faint volcanic-ash specks frozen in the glass, thick dark leading lines separating each segment, painterly tone variation within each pane. Warm sun-washed cathedral lighting from upper left on the stone arch frame only, the interior kept hot-toned. Colors permitted: charred ember-red shell, glowing amber eye, gold claw-tips, cracked-gold fracture-lines, warm-amber gild-glow, crimson background, deep-amber heat-ray, smoldering-ash flecks, dark-leading-outline. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the square canvas.
