## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with leaded stone pillars on left and right, trefoil quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels and gold leaf filigree. Subject: the Twin Grapnels — a matched PAIR of wrist-mounted harpoon-gauntlets arranged symmetrically in the center of the arch, one mirrored on the left and one on the right, each about fifty percent of the arch height. The barbed heads are opened forward, primed to launch. A glowing amber ember-gem is set into the housing of each gauntlet, the world's ember-light powering the spring. The two gauntlets angle their harpoons toward the upper-left and upper-right corners in mirror symmetry. Background: stained-glass panels in a warm-bronze and deep-amber palette radiating outward behind the twin gauntlets in a symmetrical double-burst pattern, with small harpoon-barb inlays in the outer ring. Thick dark leading lines separating glass segments, painterly stained-glass tone variation within each segment. Warm directional cathedral lighting from upper left, bronze-gold palette with amber gem accents. Colors permitted: polished-bronze, warm-gold, steel-grey-harpoon, amber-ember-glow, waxed-rope-tan, gunmetal-shadow, ivory-highlight, dark-leading-outline.
