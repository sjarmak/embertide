## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with carved-marble pillars left and right entwined with oak-leaf reliefs, trefoil quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels, hero-tier gold-leaf rose-window glowing in the upper arch. Subject: a supply-hero sentinel of Elysia — a stoic warden sworn to hold the line against the gilding, standing in a protective heraldic pose, facing forward, filling the arch's central axis at about sixty-five percent of arch height. He plants a massive rounded TOWER SHIELD at his left side, angled slightly across his body and resting its lower rim on the ground beside his boot — his left arm is clearly threaded through the shield's inner straps with the armored hand gripping the grip-bar, the bent forearm visibly bearing the shield's weight, so the shield is firmly HELD and grounded, never floating in front of the chest. His right gauntlet rests on the shield's upper rim. The shield occupies roughly thirty-five percent of the composition and bears a central faceted EMERALD jewel ringed by radiating gold-leaf filigree and bronze rivets. He wears a visored great-helm with a short horizontal slit revealing only shadow, gleaming steel-plate pauldrons and gauntlets trimmed in gold, a deep-navy tabard, and mailed greaves. No sword is shown — this is a defender, not a sword-raiser. Background: stained-glass panels in deep indigo and sapphire with cool silver-blue rays radiating outward behind the helm — no radiant halo, the composition reads protective and stable rather than triumphant — thick dark leading lines separating segments, painterly tone variation within each pane, cathedral light kept to the stone frame only so the interior stays cool-toned. Colors permitted: steel-blue plate, gold trim, deep-navy tabard, faceted-emerald shield-jewel glow, bronze rivets, gold-leaf filigree, deep-indigo background, sapphire accent, cool silver-blue rays, dark-leading-outline. Broad stable glass cells with little fussy detail; a planted three-quarter stance with the shield set to one side, not a mirror-symmetric pose. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the square canvas.
