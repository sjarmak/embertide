## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with leaded stone pillars left and right, trefoil quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels, with extra ornamental flourish and denser leading appropriate to a wild-boss raster. Subject: the Sentinel — an ancient watcher-construct centered in the arch at about sixty-five percent of arch height, a spherical stone-and-bronze core floating just above a tiled temple floor, hanging in a still watchful pose with strong radial symmetry. Its body is a riveted bronze orb girdled by carved-stone rings, six segmented metal legs curling beneath it in a radial splay like a hovering iris. A single glowing blue-cyan optic dominates the front of the orb, projecting one thin straight beam of focused light diagonally across the glass. Etched rune-channels spiral the body. Our gilding motif corrupts the construct: hairline gold fracture-lines branch across the bronze shell and the stone rings with a faint warm-amber glow leaking from the cracks, fighting against the cold cyan of the optic as though molten gold is creeping into the seams of the machine. Background: deep-teal and cyan stained-glass panels with silver-blue rays radiating outward from the glowing optic, thick dark leading lines separating each segment, painterly tone variation within each pane. Warm sun-washed cathedral lighting from upper left on the stone arch frame only, the interior kept cool-toned with the optic and gild-glow as competing light sources. Colors permitted: bronze, carved-stone-grey, teal-cyan optic, silver-blue ray, cracked-gold fracture-lines, warm-amber gild-glow, deep-teal background, dark-leading-outline. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the square canvas.
