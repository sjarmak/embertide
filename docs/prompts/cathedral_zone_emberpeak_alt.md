## Prompt

Stained glass landscape panel, 21:9 panoramic format, Elysian Cathedral card-art style: the volcanic summit of Emberpeak — a massive single conical volcanic peak dominating the center of the composition, with an open crater at the summit. SIGNATURE FEATURE — the Emberpeak SMOKE-RING HALO: a distinct free-floating TORUS-shaped ring of dark billowing smoke-clouds with bright orange-red fire and glowing embers embedded within it, orbiting HORIZONTALLY around the upper portion of the peak like a floating cloud-doughnut (a 3D cloud-torus, not flat). Crucially — this ring is a SEPARATE floating element levitating around the mountain, NOT a wreath wrapped around the crater rim. The ring encircles the peak so that part of it is visible in FRONT of the mountain silhouette (lower near-side of the torus) and part of it is visible BEHIND the peak (upper far-side of the torus). The ring is made of thick dark charcoal-black and deep-grey smoke clouds with chunks of glowing ember-orange and fire-red erupting from inside — the burning embers make it clear this is a dangerous volcanic eruption-cloud ring, not a peaceful halo. Lesser volcanic peaks silhouetted on either side of the central cone. Lava rivulets etch down the mountain flanks glowing orange-red. Across the foreground base of the mountain: SUBTLE GLOOM TRACES — asymmetric irregular ORGANIC seeping stains and flowing creeping tendrils of magical blight bleeding INTO the rocky foreground, NOT discrete round pools, NOT symmetric circular puddles, NOT concentrated puddle shapes. The gloom is rendered as uneven elongated irregular patches of fuchsia-magenta-and-blood-red hue seeping between rocks and along cracks in the ground, with varied thickness — some patches barely a stain, some thicker runnels with black-tar edges, some wisps of black tar-smoke rising only from the thicker patches. Coverage is sparse and scattered rather than dominant — the gloom is a subtle atmospheric threat layered into the landscape, not a hero element. Vary the shapes: some long meandering trails, some thin cracks, some irregular blotches with ragged asymmetric borders. A few thin gloom trickles run down the lower mountain slopes. The gloom reads as DANGEROUS DARK MAGIC distinct from the orange volcanic lava — cooler, wetter, more magenta/fuchsia rather than orange-red — but restrained in intensity, not saturating the foreground. Sky: dominant deep blood-red with molten amber streaks behind the smoke-ring transitioning to ominous charcoal-purple and gloom-fuchsia at the edges. Dark black silhouetted peaks with bronze ornament accents. Thick dark leading lines separating glass segments, painterly stained-glass tone variation within each segment. Horizontal composition with no single focal figure — the mountain silhouette + floating smoke-ring halo + gloom-trace foreground carry the atmosphere. Colors permitted: deep blood-red, molten-amber, ember-orange, fire-yellow, volcanic-lava-orange, obsidian-black, charcoal-grey-smoke, dark-grey-cloud, fuchsia-gloom, magenta-gloom, crimson-gloom-bleed, black-tar-gloom-edge, bronze-accent, dark-leading-outline. No text, no letters, no captions, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the 21:9 canvas.
