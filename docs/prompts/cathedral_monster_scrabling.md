## Prompt

Stained glass cathedral window illustration, square 1:1 format, in the Elysian Cathedral card-art style: gothic pointed arch frame with leaded stone pillars left and right entwined with emerald ivy and tiny glass-panel leaves, trefoil quatrefoil cornices in the arch spandrels, plain leaded inner border (regular-enemy treatment). Subject: the Scrabling — a small hunched root-goblin scavenger centered in the arch at about sixty percent of arch height, caught mid-charge in an aggressive crouch. Its body is a knot of woody sinew and bark-skin, a wide tusked snout below a heavy brow, long tapering ears swept back, knees high and knuckles low like a thing built to scurry. It wears scraps of stitched-bark armor and brandishes a crude knotted-root cudgel raised mid-swing in both hands. Slung at its hip is a frayed satchel of stolen trinkets — bits of bright glass and bent metal it has hoarded. Our gilding motif corrupts the creature: hairline gold fracture-lines branch across the bark-skin and the cudgel with a faint warm-amber glow leaking from the cracks, two glowing amber eye-pinpoints burning in the shadow of its brow, as though the wood is being slowly turned to gilt from within. Background: deep forest-green stained-glass panels with diagonal dappled sunbeam rays breaking through an unseen high canopy, thick dark leading lines separating each segment, painterly tone variation within each pane. Warm sun-washed cathedral lighting from upper left on the stone arch frame only, the interior kept cool forest-toned. Colors permitted: bark-brown, mossy-bronze, stitched-bark armor, knotted-root cudgel, cracked-gold fracture-lines, warm-amber gild-glow, glowing-amber eye-pinpoints, forest-green background, dappled-gold sunbeam, dark-leading-outline. No text, no letters, no captions, no watermarks, no UI overlays — just the stained-glass panel itself filling the square canvas.
